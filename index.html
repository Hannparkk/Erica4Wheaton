Why I'm Running



Throughout the years, I have sent hundreds of students to local board meetings and every time I emphasis that local government is the most impactful form of government to our daily lives. It is now time to take my own advice and get actively involved in the local decision-making process. I am proud to have raised my family in Wheaton and done my best to be a positive contributor along the way. The current board has made some very solid decisions in moving Wheaton forward as a vibrant and modern city and I’d like to help continue to move our community through the next few years and decades. Personally, I’m a cyclist and I’m excited about making Wheaton bike friendly and a place for people to choose to live and come to visit.

I’m a high school civics teacher (24 years) and Boy Scout Citizenship Merit Badge Counselor. Over the years, I have sent hundreds of kids to local board meetings. I’ve decided it’s time to take my own advice and get actively involved in the local decision making process.

I have lived in Wheaton with my family for the past 22 years. Wheaton is a great community to live in (recently ranked number one in Illinois by Time Magazine). I teach my students, and I personally believe, that local government has the greates impact on our daily lives. The current board has made some very solid decisions to move Wheaton forward as a vibrant and modern community. I want to preserve and build on what we have accomplished as citizens.

Bio



- 22-year resident of Wheaton
- Married and raised two boys through the Wheaton Public schools
- 24 years high school civics and psychology teacher - current
- Bachelor’s degree from Illinois State University in Social Science education
- Master’s degree from Northern Illinois University in Political Science through the James Madison Memorial Foundation Fellowship Award
- Elected member of Illinois Education Association Board of Directors - current and active
- Boy Scout Citizenship Merit Badge Counselor 2013-2018
- Teacher representative of Glenbard District 87 Student Performance and Achievement Committee 2013-2016
- Teacher representative of Glenbard District 87 Collaboration & Communication Committee 2010-2014
- Mentor for IL-06 Students Say Enough student community activist group - current and active
- Milton 3 Democratic Precinct Committeeman – current and active
- Member of Trinity Lutheran in Lisle - current and active
- Member League of Women Voters, Wheaton - current and active

No matter which hat Erica is wearing, she’s dedicated to being an active and informed citizen while more importantly, she’s focused on giving her students, her friends, her family and her overall community the tools and encouragement to be active citizens in their own right.
